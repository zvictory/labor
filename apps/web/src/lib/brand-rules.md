# Brand Design Rules & Typography Guidelines

This document outlines the official design language, typography guidelines, and image generation rules for the selective and niche perfume brand **Labor**. All creative design systems, digital assets, package mockups, bottle renders, and promotional materials must strictly adhere to these rules.

---

## 1. Core Typography System

The **Labor** storefront uses a curated two-font system that contrasts ultra-premium calligraphic signature styling with editorial, robust copy headlines.

### Primary Display Font: **Story Script**
* **Usage**: Brand Logo, signature highlights, calligraphic callouts.
* **Source**: [Google Fonts - Story Script](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Story+Script)
* **Aesthetic**: Elegant, fluid hand-drawn calligraphy that represents the sensory, expressive "signature" of the wearer.
* **Sizing Rule**: **Make it bold and big!** On the storefront header, it scales up to `text-5xl md:text-6xl lg:text-7xl` with wide, breathing room to command authority.

### Secondary Headings Font: **Roboto Slab**
* **Usage**: Product titles, section headers, catalog sorting panels, prices, and editorial copy.
* **Source**: [Google Fonts - Roboto Slab](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto+Slab)
* **Aesthetic**: A robust, premium slab-serif that projects structural weight, clarity, and architectural luxury (matching the look of elite niche brand catalogs).
* **Sizing Rule**: `font-bold tracking-tight text-ink` to ensure high-contrast readability against bone or dark backgrounds.

## 2. Image & Packaging Design Rules

Whenever creating visual assets, bottle renders, or generative AI images for the **Labor** brand (e.g., using Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, or DALL-E), apply these parameters:

### Typography on Bottles & Packaging
> [!IMPORTANT]
> Any visual representation of the "Labor" logo on physical products (perfume bottles, custom boxes, labels, bags) **MUST** utilize the calligraphic, cursive forms modeled directly on the **Story Script** font. No block sans-serif logos are allowed for the brand name.

* **Scale**: The brand name "Labor" should be printed in a **larger, prominent size** relative to other details.
* **Layout**: Place "Labor" in cursive script at the top-center of the label, followed by the fragrance name in a typewriter-like monospace font (e.g. `SANTAL 33` or `THE NOIR 29`) or a clean slab-serif, establishing a distinct contrast between high-fashion calligraphy and clinical laboratory precision.
* **Subtexts**: Volumetric labels (e.g., `100 ml 3.4 FL.OZ.`, `Eau de Parfum`) must be in a small, clean, high-tracking monospace sans-serif at the bottom of the label.

### Color Palette (Aesthetics)
The brand identity is constructed around a strict, curated monochrome and earth-tone palette:
1. **Bone** (`#FAF8F4`): The primary background, representing raw textured paper, light linens, and clean limestone.
2. **Ink** (`#1A1714`): The main typographic color, a deep charcoal-black mimicking natural oak gall ink.
3. **Brass** (`#8B6F47`): The metallic highlight, representing aged brass caps, golden perfume oils, and luxury detailing.

### Styling & Composition
* **Inspiration**: Draw inspiration from high-fashion houses like **Louis Vuitton Perfumes**, **Jo Malone London**, and **Le Labo**.
* **Scene**: Bottled products should be photographed or rendered resting on raw organic materials—rough-cut travertine stone blocks, weathered driftwood, raw sandalwood branches, or dry vetiver roots.
* **Lighting**: Use high-contrast natural daylight casting deep, geometric shadows. Avoid sterile studio box lights; favor the warm sun filtering through a high loft window.
